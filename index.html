Off the Beaten Path

From pho and sushi to texmex and BBQ, from wine and beer to tea and coffee; Sacramento is host to a bevy of underrated food establishments and breweries. In the interest of limiting my list, I will only list a few of my favorite places that are more difficult to get to as they aren't located near midtown or good public transit.

First, Pho Bac Hoa Viet located along Bradshaw just south of Folsom. This restaurant has a splendor of options of Thai, Vietnamese, and similar popular dishes. Their roll your own spring roll special is something different to try if you have never used rice paper before. Careful you don't stick it all in a mess!
Link to their menu: Menu | Pho Bac Hoa Viet

Next, Janoi's App Khao, Papaya and Noodle Shack located south off the 80 at Watt and Auburn. This place is home to wonderfully fresh dishes, treated in a way that leaves them more tender and full of spice. Last time there, I ordered this beef and lemongrass salad that was almost rare but so tender and delish. Even after taking some home and saving it for the next day, the meat had just marinated all-night and had just steeped in flavor!
Link to their menu: Menu | Janoi's App Khao

Finally, I'd like to give a special mention to all the local sandwich shops (not including Subway). Nothing beats a good sandwich for eating on the run and here in Sacramento, almost every shop I've been to knows how to do it just right! So don't sub out a good sandwich, the local shops know how to slice it.
